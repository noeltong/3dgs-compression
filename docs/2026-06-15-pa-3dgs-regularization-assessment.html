Technical assessment for a single-volume photoacoustic 3D Gaussian Splatting compression model • generated June 15, 2026

Regularization Methods for Photoacoustic 3DGS Compression

This report evaluates photoacoustic- and acoustic-motivated regularization methods for a coordinate-based 3D Gaussian Splatting codec that fits a single reconstructed 3D volume from sampled coordinate-intensity pairs. The ranking is optimized for rate-distortion behavior at fixed or near-fixed bit budget, not for unconstrained reconstruction quality alone.

Literature-grounded PA/PAT/PAM focused Compression-oriented Training-only regularization preferred

1. Executive Summary

Taking PAT into account, the most promising methods for this codec are the ones that remain physically and statistically sensible for signed measurement-domain signals and for reconstructed image-domain volumes: anisotropic or directional TV, Gaussian sparsity / pruning regularization, directional sparse transforms such as wavelets or curvelets, MAP-aware edge or spectral losses when projected-view quality matters, and bandwidth / axial spectral regularization.

The common mechanism is simple: under a fixed quantized payload, the model should spend its Gaussian degrees of freedom on vessel boundaries, strong absorbers, and meaningful depth structure rather than on signed cancellations, tiny oscillatory artifacts, or redundant overlapping blobs.

Evidence boundary. Claims about PA relevance are based on PA/OA/PAT/PAM reconstruction and inverse-problem literature. PAT time-series or sinogram-domain measurements are signed acoustic waveforms; non-negativity is a prior on the reconstructed p0 image, not on the measured pressure traces. Claims about compression benefit for this particular coordinate-based Gaussian codec are model-specific inference unless explicitly tied to 3DGS compression work.

3. Top 5 Recommendations

1. Anisotropic / Directional TV

Mechanism. Penalize spatial gradients with directional weighting rather than isotropic smoothing.

Photoacoustic relevance. Vascular PA structure is anisotropic; PAT/OAT reconstruction literature explicitly studies TV-style priors for sparse and noisy acquisition.

Literature-backed. Adaptive anisotropic TV preserved complex vessel boundaries better than TV-L1 in optoacoustic model-based inversion.

Model-specific inference. Mild directional TV should remove small noisy oscillations that otherwise consume Gaussian capacity while keeping elongated vessels sharp.

Acts on. Predicted volume, optionally MAP image.

Effects. PSNR/SSIM: small to moderate gain; vessel preservation: good; equal-quality compression: moderate gain; training stability: good.

Risk. Thin capillaries can be oversmoothed if the weight is too large.

Confidence: High

2. Gaussian Sparsity / Pruning / Quant-Aware Regularization

Mechanism. Penalize active amplitudes, encourage redundant Gaussians to die, and favor quantization-friendly parameter distributions.

Photoacoustic relevance. Indirect rather than physics-native.

Literature-backed. 3DGS compression work such as LightGaussian shows pruning and compact parameterization can preserve quality under smaller payloads.

Model-specific inference. This is probably the strongest direct lever on rate-distortion for your codec because fewer active Gaussians mean fewer useful bits spent.

Acts on. Gaussian parameters.

Effects. PSNR: neutral to small gain if paired with better priors; equal-quality compression: high upside; training stability: fair.

Risk. Premature pruning can underfit diffuse or low-contrast absorbers.

Confidence: Medium

3. Directional Wavelet / Curvelet Sparsity

Mechanism. Penalize high-frequency transform coefficients, especially in directional frames that match vessel geometry.

Photoacoustic relevance. Curvelet and wavelet sparsity have direct PAT compressed-sensing and reconstruction support.

Literature-backed. PAT works report sparse curvelet representations for image or data domains and better reconstruction from reduced measurements.

Model-specific inference. Patchwise transform sparsity should bias the learned field toward representations that a compact Gaussian basis can model efficiently.

Acts on. Predicted volume patches or MAP patches.

Effects. MAP quality: strong gain potential; vessel preservation: strong if transform matches anatomy; compression: moderate upside.

Risk. Implementation complexity and mismatch when anatomy is not strongly tubular.

Confidence: Medium

4. MAP Multi-Scale Gradient + Spectral Loss

Mechanism. Match edges and spectrum in projected MAP images at multiple scales.

Photoacoustic relevance. PAM studies often emphasize MAP views because limited axial resolution makes projection quality operationally important.

Literature-backed. The relevance is indirect; the literature mostly motivates the importance of MAP quality and axial bandwidth rather than this exact loss.

Model-specific inference. This would require adding a new training-only projection loss path if you decide to optimize rate-distortion toward the MAP views users inspect.

Acts on. Projected MAP image.

Effects. MAP quality: strong gain; 3D PSNR: neutral to small gain; compression: small to moderate upside.

Risk. Can produce good-looking MAP projections while slightly harming full 3D fidelity if overweighted.

Confidence: Medium

5. Bandwidth / Axial Spectral Regularization

Mechanism. Encourage reconstructed patches to match the target's axial or temporal-frequency content without imposing sign constraints.

Photoacoustic relevance. PAT image quality is strongly shaped by acoustic bandwidth, detector response, and anisotropic axial resolution.

Literature-backed. PAT reconstruction and beamforming literature repeatedly ties axial sharpness and artifact behavior to spectral support and detector bandwidth.

Model-specific inference. A mild spectral prior is more PAT-compatible than positivity when the training targets retain signed or oscillatory structure.

Acts on. Predicted volume patches.

Effects. Axial sharpness: moderate gain; 3D PSNR: small gain potential; compression: modest upside if high-frequency noise is suppressed.

Risk. Overweighting can hallucinate or over-preserve ringing.

Confidence: Medium
Conditional method. Entropy-style positivity remains literature-backed for reconstructed non-negative p0 images, but it should not be treated as a generic PAT prior. It is inappropriate for raw PAT sinograms, boundary pressure traces, or other targets where signed oscillations are physically meaningful.

4. Probably Worth Trying First

  • Directional TV on sampled 3D patches as the first regularizer that keeps training fully volumetric and stays valid for signed PAT-like targets.
  • Mild Gaussian pruning or dead-Gaussian regularization after a warm-up period.
  • MAP multi-scale gradient plus MAP FFT magnitude matching if projected-view quality is operationally important.
  • Axial spectral matching on sampled 3D patches if the target quality bottleneck is depth blur or bandwidth loss.

5. Interesting But High-Risk

  • Joint intensity plus second-derivative sparsity from reduced-data PAT. It is PA-specific and elegant, but the optimization landscape is less forgiving.
  • Vesselness or Hessian-based tubularity loss on MAP patches. It may preserve vessels, but it can also bias the model toward artificial tubular structures.
  • Curvelet regularization if transform implementation becomes expensive or unstable.
  • Gaussian repulsion or overlap regularization to reduce redundant blobs. This is promising but under-validated in PA compression.

6. Probably Not Worth It Here

  • Full acoustic forward-model consistency unless raw time-series data and acquisition geometry are available during codec training.
  • Speed-of-sound heterogeneity or detector-response calibration losses for the current voxel-only learning setup.
  • Low-rank, nonlocal self-similarity, or dictionary-learning priors as first-line methods for a single reconstructed volume.
  • Limited-view-specific priors unless the training volume obviously retains limited-angle artifacts.
  • Soft positivity when the supervision target is raw PAT pressure data, a PAT sinogram volume, or any reconstruction where signed lobes are part of the desired signal.

7. Concrete Implementation Suggestions

The model already has direct insertion points for training-only losses. The main loss is assembled in run_lib.py around voxel MSE, and Gaussian parameters are also easy to regularize because centers, scales, and intensities are exposed in the model parameter activation path.

Conditional Positivity for p0-like Targets

Loss.

L_pos = mean(softplus(-V_hat / tau))

Plug-in point. Add to the total loss alongside voxel MSE only when the target semantics are reconstructed non-negative initial pressure.

Needs extra data? No.

Best use. Pair with amplitude sparsity so the model cannot cheaply rely on positive-negative cancellation; do not use it for raw PAT time-series, sinograms, or intentionally bipolar targets.

Amplitude Sparsity / Group Sparsity

Loss.

L_group = sum_i |a_i|

or

L_group = sum_i sqrt(a_i^2 + eta * ||s_i||_2^2)

Plug-in point. Gaussian parameter regularizer.

Needs extra data? No.

Best use. Warm up without sparsity, then ramp it in.

Directional TV

Loss.

L_dTV = lambda_xy * ||grad_xy(V_hat_patch)||_1 + lambda_z * ||d_z(V_hat_patch)||_1

Plug-in point. Sample a dense 3D cube and apply finite differences.

Needs extra data? No.

Best use. Keep weights mild and possibly depth-dependent.

MAP Multi-Scale Gradient + Spectrum

Loss.

L_map = sum_s w_s ||grad(M(D_s(V_hat))) - grad(M(D_s(V)))||_1 + lambda_f ||log(1 + |F(M(V_hat))|) - log(1 + |F(M(V))|)||_1

Plug-in point. Add a new training-only projection branch if you choose to optimize projected views.

Needs extra data? No.

Best use. Only worth adding if you intentionally expand the objective beyond pure voxelwise reconstruction.

Wavelet / Curvelet Sparsity

Loss.

L_wav = lambda * sum_highpass |W(V_hat_patch)|

Plug-in point. Apply to 3D patches or MAP patches.

Needs extra data? No.

Best use. Start with wavelets before curvelets for implementation simplicity.

Axial Spectral Matching

Loss.

L_zfft = ||omega(k_z) * (|F_z(V_hat_patch)| - |F_z(V_patch)|)||_1

Plug-in point. Dense 3D patch branch.

Needs extra data? No.

Best use. Use a tapered mid/high-frequency weight rather than uniformly penalizing all frequencies.

Code-fit note. The current training loop is pure voxelwise reconstruction. New regularizers remain mechanically straightforward: add volume or projection loss terms beside voxel MSE, and add Gaussian-parameter penalties from the parameter tensors before backpropagation. The only one that needs target-semantic gating is positivity, because it is valid for reconstructed p0-like images but not for signed PAT measurement-domain targets.

8. Gaps in the Literature

  • There is very little direct literature on rate-distortion-aware regularization for single-volume implicit PA codecs.
  • Most PA regularization studies operate on raw acoustic measurements with an explicit forward operator, not on already reconstructed volumes.
  • The applicability of non-negativity depends strongly on target semantics: reconstructed p0 images differ materially from signed PAT pressure time-series or sinogram-domain data.
  • There is almost no published work combining photoacoustic physics priors with Gaussian-primitive sparsity and quantization.
  • PAM papers often optimize visualization or segmentation quality, not payload efficiency under a strict bit budget.
  • I did not find strong evidence that low-rank, dictionary, or nonlocal priors are especially attractive for this exact static single-volume compression setting.

10. Prioritized Experimental Plan

Three Easiest First

  1. Directional TV plus mild amplitude sparsity.
  2. Gaussian group sparsity or dead-Gaussian regularization after warm-up.
  3. MAP spectral loss or axial spectral loss on sampled patches.

Two Highest-Upside

  1. Directional wavelet or curvelet sparsity.
  2. Gaussian group sparsity plus mild redundancy reduction.

Two to Avoid for Now

  1. Full acoustic forward-model consistency.
  2. Soft positivity on raw PAT or other signed-pressure targets.
